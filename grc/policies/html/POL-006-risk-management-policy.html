1. Purpose

This policy establishes the organizational approach to managing information security risks. It defines the risk assessment methodology, risk tolerance, treatment options, and monitoring requirements that guide all security decision-making. Risk management is not a one-time activity — it is a continuous process embedded in all aspects of organizational operations.

This policy implements the NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF) as defined in NIST SP 800-37 Rev 2 and the risk assessment methodology in NIST SP 800-30 Rev 1.

2. Risk Tolerance Statement

The organization maintains a moderate risk tolerance for information security risks consistent with a FIPS 199 Moderate system categorization. Risks with a residual score exceeding 12 (on the 5×5 matrix) require executive approval for risk acceptance. Risks scored 20 or above (Critical) shall not be accepted without board-level approval and must have active mitigation in progress.

3. Governing NIST Controls

RA-3
Risk Assessment: The organization conducts an assessment of risk, including the likelihood and magnitude of harm, from the unauthorized access, use, disclosure, disruption, modification, or destruction of the information system and the information it processes, stores, or transmits. Risk assessments are conducted annually and updated upon significant system or environment changes.
CA-7
Continuous Monitoring: The organization develops a continuous monitoring strategy and implements a continuous monitoring program that includes ongoing security control assessments, ongoing security status monitoring, and reporting the security and privacy posture of organizational systems.

4. Risk Assessment Methodology

4.1 Risk Identification

Risks shall be identified through multiple channels: vulnerability scanning results; penetration testing findings; threat intelligence analysis; security incident post-mortems; audit findings; control assessments; and personnel-reported concerns. All identified risks shall be entered into the Risk Register within 5 business days of identification.

4.2 Risk Scoring Matrix

Risks are scored using a 5×5 likelihood × impact matrix. Likelihood and impact are each scored 1–5, and the risk score is the product (1–25):

4.3 Risk Treatment Options

For each identified risk, one of the following treatment options shall be selected and documented:

  • Mitigate: Implement controls to reduce likelihood or impact to an acceptable level — preferred option for high and critical risks
  • Transfer: Transfer risk to a third party (e.g., cyber insurance, vendor contract) — appropriate for some financial and operational risks
  • Accept: Formally accept the risk when cost of mitigation exceeds benefit — requires documented approval proportional to risk level
  • Avoid: Eliminate the risk by discontinuing the activity that creates it — appropriate when risk cannot be adequately mitigated

5. NIST RMF Lifecycle Integration

The organization follows the full NIST RMF lifecycle for all information systems:

  1. Categorize: Classify the system per FIPS 199 (Moderate impact baseline)
  2. Select: Choose controls from NIST SP 800-53 Rev 5 Moderate baseline
  3. Implement: Deploy controls; document implementation in System Security Plan (SSP)
  4. Assess: Test control effectiveness through assessments and audits
  5. Authorize: Make formal risk acceptance decisions; issue ATO where applicable
  6. Monitor: Continuously monitor control effectiveness; update risk posture

6. Risk Register Maintenance

The Risk Register is the authoritative record of all identified organizational risks. It shall be maintained as a living document and reviewed monthly. Each risk entry shall include: risk ID; title and description; threat source; vulnerability exploited; likelihood score; impact score; risk score and level; current controls; treatment decision; owner; due date; status; and residual risk after controls.

7. Risk Monitoring and Reporting

  • Risk register reviewed: monthly by ISO
  • High/Critical risks reviewed: weekly until resolved or accepted
  • Executive risk briefing: quarterly
  • Annual full risk assessment: covers all in-scope systems
  • Event-triggered re-assessment: upon significant incidents, system changes, or new threat intelligence
